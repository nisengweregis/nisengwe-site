Professional experience
  • Graduate Research Assistant, University of Tennessee May, 2019

    I am currently working with the Smith Center for International Agriculture as a graduate research assistant. My particular interest is to use data science techniques to answer questions related to natural resources in the Rwanda-based food security project

  • National Consultant (Natural Resources), Indufor January, 2018

    As a consultant, I was working on the IFC-supported "Natural Capital" project with Indufor. That project aimed at analyzing the dependencies and impacts of the beverage industry in Rwanda on natural capital. Indufor is an independent international consulting company based in Finland that provides advisory services to a wide range of private and public sector clients in forestry, forest industry and sustainable natural resource management. As a national consultant, I was in charge of coordinating the program and facilitating the activities carried out in Rwanda.

  • Program Associate, One Acre Fund, Rwanda May, 2016

    Farming is the dominant economic activity of the world’s poor. One Acre Fund (OAF) makes that activity significantly more productive. OAF's services include financing for farm inputs, distribution of seed and fertilizer, training on agricultural techniques, and market facilitation to maximize profits from harvest sales. As program associate, my project portfolio included farmer protection and team development.

Academic experience
  • Ph.D., Natural Resources, University of Tennessee Ongoing ...

    I am interested in looking at the environment, natural resources, and sustainability and how they relate to poverty alleviation and food security in developing countries. More particularly, I am interested in understanding and predicting human behaviors towards natural resources and the environment. To do this, I heavily rely on modern data science techniques such as deep learning

  • MSc, Natural Resources and Environment, Michigan State University May, 2016

    From hard-core science, I moved to management and policy science of the natural resources and environment spectrum. On this end, my interest revolved around climate change and human dimensions. Particularly, I was interested in understanding how farmers in Nyabihu district (northern Rwanda) cope with climate-change-induced extreme events, by understanding their risk perceptions and attitudes. This constituted the basis for my thesis that I successfully completed with invaluable help from my main professor, Dr. John Kerr. I remain indebted to him.

  • BSc, Environmental Chemistry, University of Rwanda February, 2012

    I focused on finding innovative chemical solutions to respond to environmental issues. In particular, my final-year project aimed at addressing pollution based on polystyrene waste. My classmate and I, with help from our professor, we successfully produced varnish from polystryrene-based waste, thus reducing this waste in nature and then producing useful product out of it.
